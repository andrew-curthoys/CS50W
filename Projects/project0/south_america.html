South America

Me Gusta Mucho (Eu Gosto Muito)

I have had the pleasure to take two trips to South America, one to Chile and another to Brazil. The first trip was a gift from my uncle, who gave each of my brothers a trip as a gift for our high school graduation. This was the same uncle who, along with my dad, taught me how to ski. Naturally our trip was to Chile to go skiing in July.

We flew into Santiago, which was an amazing and beautiful city. From there we rented a car and made the drive up to Portillo, in the heart of the Andes Mountains right on the border of Argentina. We spent 4-5 days skiing, some of which we had to wait out because the snow was so heavy and the weather was so windy. Luckily the hotel had a heated pool, a hot tub, and plenty of dining and recreation activities to pass the time.

When we were able to ski, the conditions were incredible. The fresh snow was amazing and the mountain was not crowded at all. My uncle, cousin, and I bombed up and down the mountain all day. It was some amazing skiing. After skiing, we drove across the country to the coast and the towns of Valparaiso and Vina del Mar. We stayed at a hotel that was built on top of some jagged rocks jutting out into the Pacific Ocean and toured around the town most days and took a tour of the famous poet Pablo Neruda. We even took a quick dip in the ocean, which was probably the coldest water I have ever felt in my entire life. Unfortunately I wasn't able to find any photos from that trip, although I'm sure we have some somewhere.

My second trip to South America was to Brazil for my friend's wedding. This was a friend I grew up with in Tulsa, who met a Brazilian girl while attending the US Military Academy at West Point. She is from a city called Belo Horizonte, the fourth largest city in Brazil. When we arrived we were immediately whisked to a soccer game by the bride's brother and his friends, where we got the full Brazilian soccer experience. I still check in on their local team, Cruzeiro, from time to time and have a team scarf hanging in my house.

The next few days we spent touring around Belo Horizonte, seeing the sights and enjoying the delicious food and culture that Brazil has to offer. The wedding turned out wonderfully, with drinks and dancing lasting well into the night. Brazilians certainly know how to dance!

After Belo, a group of friends from Tulsa made the trip to Rio for a few days. Affectionately called Cidade Maravilhosa (the Marvelous City), Rio is a spectacular place. I had made a list of sights to see and things to do in the city while we were there, but we didn't get to all of them due to the lure of the beach, with its delicious pork sandwiches and caipirinhas (the national drink of Brazil).

We did, however, make it to the Cristo Redentor (Christ the Redeemer), the famous statue of Jesus on a mountaintop in the center of the city. The rest of the trip we spent on the beach and enjoying the outstanding food and drink that Rio had to offer. We even had a drink at the bar where the song The Girl From Ipanema was written. It was a spectacular trip and I would love to go back to take a tram to the top of Sugarloaf Mountain and see a soccer game at the Maracana Stadium, among other things.
