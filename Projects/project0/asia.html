Asia, but barely

The only time I have stepped foot on Asian soil was my trip to Turkey en route to Egypt for work. We had meetings in Cairo so a few co-workers and I decided to spend some time in Istanbul and Ismir before getting to Cairo. Our trip to Istanbul was amazing, and you can read more about that in the Europe section. The only Asian part of Istanbul we went to was a small fort at the northern mouth of the Bosphorous Strait, right on the edge of the Black Sea. It was a pretty neat tour, but a short one. The best part of that was probably the ferry ride from Istanbul up to the fort, which provided great views of the strait and the immense suspension bridges that spanned it

The main part of our Asian experience was in Ismir and the nearby ruins of Ephesus. Ismir is a great seaside town on the Aegean Sea, with all the trappings of a Mediterranean city. We spent some time in Ismir and had some great meals and met some very nice people.

While we were in Ismir we took a trip to the ancient ruins of Ephesus. This was a great experience where we were able to see the city rebuilt as it was centuries ago. There were numerous large structures that had been beautifully restored and a couple amazing amphitheaters that were quite stunning. It was well worth the trip.
