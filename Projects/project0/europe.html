Europe

Europe, kinda?

The only time I have set foot on European soil was a trip I took for work a few years ago where we made a stop in Istanbul. This is regarded as the dividing line between Europe and Asia, so technically I was in Europe and Asia on the same short trip. Although I was just barely in Europe I still thoroughly enjoyed Istanbul.

Before the trip I read a book on the history of Istanbul, which was known for much of history as Constantinople. The city is perfectly situated to be defended against foreign invaders, which it successfully did for over a millennium, until Mehmed the Conquerer of the Ottoman Empire successfully conquered the city in 1453. Until then the city was the capital of the Eastern Holy Roman Empire and has always been at the center of world trade due to its geographic advantages. As such, the city has a remarkably rich history, stretching back thousands of years.

Some highlights included the ruins of the central coliseum, the Basilica Cistern, the Blue Mosque, the sultan's palace, the Grand Bazaar, and my favorite place, the Hagia Sophia. This was built in 537 and stands 180 ft tall in the center of the city. It was built by the Byzantine Emperor Justinian as a Byzantine Catholic church, however it was transformed into a mosque when the city was conquered by the Ottomans.
